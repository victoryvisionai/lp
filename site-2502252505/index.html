Our Family Story

From the bustling streets of Brooklyn to the tranquil gardens of Tokyo, our family embodies the beautiful fusion of two rich cultures.

Brooklyn Beginnings

1995 - 2005

Our story began in Brooklyn, where Jamaican rhythms met Japanese traditions in the melting pot of New York City. The vibrant Caribbean community and the growing Japanese diaspora created the perfect backdrop for cultural exchange.

A Union of Cultures

2008

Our parents' wedding blended Jamaican and Japanese traditions - a reggae processional followed by a sake ceremony. The celebration featured jerk chicken alongside sushi, steel drum music with taiko drums, creating a truly unique fusion.

Growing Roots in Two Worlds

2010 - Present

With homes in both Brooklyn and Tokyo, our family thrives at the intersection of these cultures. Summers in Jamaica, spring in Japan for cherry blossom season, and year-round celebrations that honor both heritages equally.

Cultural Fusion

Discover how we blend the vibrant energy of Jamaica with the refined elegance of Japan in our daily lives.

Music & Dance

Our family gatherings feature a unique blend of reggae beats with traditional Japanese folk music. Dancehall moves meet the precision of bon odori, creating a celebration that's both energetic and graceful.

Celebrations

We celebrate both Jamaican Independence Day and Japanese Obon festival with equal enthusiasm. Our hybrid celebrations include flying kites with Jamaican colors and eating jerk chicken bento boxes.

Language & Values

Our home resonates with Jamaican Patois, Japanese, and English. We've blended the Jamaican concept of "Irie" (everything's alright) with the Japanese "Omotenashi" (selfless hospitality) to create our unique family philosophy.
